From Nepal to the Nordics: My Journey of Dreams and Discovery

Hi, I'm Bhuwan Paudel. I was born in a small village called Karkineta, hidden in the mountains of Nepal. Growing up there was peaceful and beautiful, surrounded by nature’s calm, which sparked my love for adventure and exploring from a young age.

For high school, I moved to Kathmandu, Nepal's bustling capital city of Nepal. It was a significant change — challenging, overwhelming at times, but also exciting. Kathmandu exposed me to a world much bigger than my village, full of diversity and energy. Later, I found myself studying Software Engineering in Pokhara, a city known for its serene lakes and towering mountains. This reminded me a lot of home and helped me stay grounded during those tough years.

After graduating, I worked in a private institution and then at an engineering college. Those experiences taught me a lot about responsibility, leadership, and the value of education. But deep down, I have always dreamed of studying abroad, experiencing new cultures firsthand, and broadening my horizons beyond Nepal.

That dream took me to Oslo, Norway — a place where the beauty of nature felt familiar, yet the long, dark winters posed an unexpected challenge. Living there pushed me to develop resilience and determination. I learned to adapt to new environments, embrace uncertainty, and appreciate small moments of joy, like the glow of the Northern Lights or the tranquility of a quiet snowfall.

Completing my master's degree from the University of Oslo (UiO) was a proud achievement, but my ambitions didn’t stop there. I set my sights on earning a PhD to deepen my knowledge and make at least a few meaningful contributions to my field. This goal led me to the Blekinge Institute of Technology (BTH) in Sweden, a smaller and quieter place compared to Oslo but big enough for growth and discovery.

Over the past six years, my journey has been about more than just traveling from one place to another. It has been a path of self-discovery, where I've met incredible people from around the world and immersed myself in diverse cultures. I've savored various foods and traditions that have enriched my life. Each experience has played a significant role in shaping who I am today—an open-minded, curious, and grateful individual.

Reflecting on my journey from the mountains of Nepal to the Europe, I see a narrative filled with dreams pursued, challenges confronted, and countless moments of learning and joy. I am grateful for every step I've taken and look forward to the adventures that lie ahead, eager to make a positive impact in my community and the world.
