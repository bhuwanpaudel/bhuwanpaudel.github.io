About Me

I am a doctoral candidate in Software Engineering at the Software Engineering Research Lab (SERL), Blekinge Institute of Technology (BTH), Karlskrona, Sweden. My research focuses on data-driven and sustainable software product development, aiming to optimize internal software quality while ensuring continuous delivery of customer value. I am particularly passionate about understanding how software development organizations can measure and integrate external customer value with internal process and product-related metrics. The overarching goal is to support organizations in effectively balancing resource allocation to enhance both asset quality and value delivery.

I earned my Bachelor of Software Engineering degree from Nepal and then moved to Oslo, Norway, for my Master's degree in Informatics: Programming and Systems Architecture from the University of Oslo. After completing my Master's, I relocated to Sweden, where I am currently pursuing my PhD. My broader research interests span empirical software engineering, technical debt, software architecture, data-driven analysis, generative AI, and mining software repositories.

Outside the academic world, I consider myself a casual backpacker and nature lover, often exploring mountains and trails. While I may look like a serious hiker, I'm mostly in it for the snacks, photos, and amazing views. I also enjoy playing and watching football.
